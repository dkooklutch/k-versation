# Reference and design analysis

Recorded July 19, 2026 after inspecting By Monolog and Neue Montreal at desktop and mobile-responsive structures.

## Observed visual language

- Display category: tightly spaced contemporary neo-grotesque, often monumental and used as composition rather than decoration.
- Supporting category: small uppercase functional labels and restrained body copy; K-VERSATION adds an editorial serif for papers and reflective writing.
- Display scale: approximately 72–220 px across common desktop widths; body copy 15–20 px; labels 10–12 px.
- Display leading: approximately .76–.96; body leading 1.4–1.65; labels use .08–.15 em tracking.
- Margins: approximately 20 px mobile, 40–64 px desktop. Vertical sections often occupy most of a viewport.
- Layout: ruled asymmetric grids, large image fields, text/image counterweights, and deliberate shifts in density.
- Image ratios: cinematic 16:10 fields paired with narrow 4:5 editorial covers. Crops are flush and rectangular.
- Borders: single low-contrast 1 px rules; minimal radius and no ornamental shadows.
- Motion: 350–1100 ms with ease-out/cubic timing; text masking, image scale, menu takeover, and controlled opening choreography.
- Responsive transformation: headings remain assertive, navigation becomes a full-screen index, ruled grids simplify, and images lead rather than every desktop column merely stacking.

## K-VERSATION tokens

- `ink #161512`, `paper #f0eee8`, `persimmon #a83c2d`, muted ink `#6d6a62`
- Manrope display and interface type; Cormorant Garamond editorial type
- Fluid page margin `clamp(20px, 3.2vw, 60px)`
- Primary easing `cubic-bezier(.16,1,.3,1)`
- The hyphen is the bridge/pause/exchange device across the wordmark, opening sequence, artwork, and micro-rules.

## Originality boundary

No reference logos, source code, copy, client assets, videos, or page compositions are used. The three editorial images are original generated assets representing paper, distance, and a connecting thread.
