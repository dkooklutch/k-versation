# K-VERSATION identity

The wordmark uses **Syne**, an OFL-licensed display face designed by Bonjour Monde and Lucas Descroix. Its unconventional proportions and open, editorial presence make the large title recognizable without borrowing another publication's proprietary typography. The included `app/fonts/LICENSE-syne.txt` preserves the license.

The custom bridge replaces a typed hyphen: two endpoints connected by a rule represent Korea and the wider world, a pause, and an active exchange. The same geometry appears in the compact K–V mark, favicon, footer and loading screen.

The palette is warm paper (`#f2f0e8`), near-black ink (`#15171a`), graphite stone and a restrained editorial navy (`#2d4a68`). Public imagery is intentionally neutralized. Red is reserved only for destructive administration and critical errors.

The opening geometry is simplified from the public-domain Natural Earth country boundary dataset (1:10m cultural vectors), retaining the South Korean mainland and Jeju. It is rendered as multiple SVG outline layers in CSS 3D perspective to provide depth with no WebGL dependency.
